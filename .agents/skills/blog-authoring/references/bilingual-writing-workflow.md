# Bilingual Writing Workflow

Use this optional workflow when the goal is to improve English writing, not just finish a draft.

## What this workflow is for

- Preserve idea density with a Chinese thinking draft
- Train direct English expression with an English-only pass
- Use AI as a reference and reviewer rather than the default writer
- End with an author-owned final version and reusable writing notes

## Loop for the author

1. Capture the idea in Chinese, English, or mixed notes
2. Write a Chinese thinking draft
3. Hide it and write an English thinking draft from memory and understanding
4. Ask AI for an English reference version based on the Chinese draft
5. Compare the Chinese draft, the author's English draft, and the AI reference
6. Synthesize a final English version
7. Extract reusable expressions, sentence patterns, transitions, or structural moves

## Guardrails

- Do not look at the AI reference before the English thinking draft exists
- Do not treat the AI version as the answer to copy
- Rephrase when direct translation feels hard
- Prefer selective synthesis over full replacement

## When to use it

- The user explicitly wants English-writing practice
- The piece is a high-value English draft, especially on `tech`
- The task is about comparison, rewrite strategy, or phrase collection

## When to skip it

- The user only wants proofreading
- The draft is short and the loop adds more process than value
- The draft is already stable and only needs targeted editing

## AI's role

- Help structure the Chinese draft without forcing English too early
- Review the author's English draft with `english-review`
- Generate the reference version only after the author's English pass exists
- Summarize reusable differences in expression, sentence pattern, paragraph flow, and voice
- Help turn the comparison into short writing notes
